# Activation Moment

The activation moment is the point at which your Context-OS workspace becomes fully operational. It is defined as the approval of your first briefing.

## Why approval, not arrival

You might expect activation to happen when you first log in, or when you finish connecting your integrations, or when the first briefing draft appears on screen. None of those moments is activation.

Activation is the approval of the first briefing because approval is the first human governance action. Until a human has reviewed an AI-generated draft and confirmed it accurately represents the current state, there is no validated organizational memory to build on. The approved briefing is the first entry in that memory. Everything that follows references it.

This design reflects a core principle of Context-OS: the AI generates, the human governs. Surfacing the draft is a necessary step, but it is not sufficient. The governance action is what creates the record.

## What changes after activation

Before activation, Context-OS is in onboarding mode. You have access to the setup flow and the first briefing review, but not to the full product.

After activation, the full navigation becomes available. This includes the Galaxy view of your initiative network, the Topology view of system dependencies, and the Decisions view of the organizational memory graph. You also gain access to recurring briefing scheduling, which automates the cadence of future draft generation and review cycles.

The second briefing is generated against the context established by the first. Each subsequent one adds to the accumulating memory. The quality and specificity of briefings increases as the graph grows.

## Activation is per organization

Each organization has its own activation moment. If your organization has multiple Strategic Operators, the first approved briefing activates the workspace for all of them simultaneously. Individual user sign-ups do not create separate activation events.
