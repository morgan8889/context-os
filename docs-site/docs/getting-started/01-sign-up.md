# Sign Up

Your weekly briefing takes roughly an hour to write. You pull from Jira, check the GitHub PR queue, skim Slack for decisions that slipped through, and assemble a narrative that explains what changed, what is blocked, and what needs your attention. Then you do it again next week.

Context-OS drafts that briefing for you in five minutes. You review, edit inline if anything needs adjustment, and approve. That is it.

## What happens at sign-up

After creating your account with your work email, you will see a discovery survey before anything else. The survey has one question: which part of your week would you most want to change?

The options reflect the five most common problems Context-OS addresses:

- Preparing weekly or bi-weekly briefings
- Tracking dependencies between teams or systems
- Retrieving decisions that were made in Slack or documents
- Reducing architecture review cycle time
- Something else

Your answer shapes how Context-OS presents information to you from day one. It also helps the team understand which workflows matter most to people in roles like yours. There is a free-text field if none of the options match your situation precisely.

The survey takes under a minute. Once you submit it, you move directly to connecting your integrations.

## Why the question matters

Context-OS pulls from Jira, GitHub, and Slack to understand what your team is working on, what has shipped, what is at risk, and what decisions have been made. Before it can surface anything useful, it needs to know which of those sources you are using and which projects matter to you.

The discovery survey is the first step in that process. It is not gatekeeping or personalization theater. It is the foundation for a first briefing that is actually relevant to your week, not a generic summary of everything.

## After the survey

You will be taken to the integration connection step. Nothing in the survey is locked in permanently. You can update your preferences later from your account settings.

If you leave during or after the survey and return later, Context-OS saves your progress. You pick up where you left off.
